Harbours of Hope: Mediterranean Migration Crisis

Welcome to the Harbours of Hope Project

The Mediterranean migration crisis plays a very big role in shaping the EU and it's policies. There is a lot of data and visualisations focused on the the issue as a whole but often migrant youth are overlooked. By visualizing migrant youth-related data, we hope to broaden our understanding of how young people are experiencing the crisis. Through the use of the interactive charts and graphs on this site, their challenges and current experiences can be considered in relation to the journeys they have undetaken. This project is part of our coursework in Interactive Information Visualization, and our main goal is to contribute meaningfully to the understanding of migration dynamics.

About the Visualizations

Our visualizations provide a comparative analysis of educational achievements and employment statuses among young migrants across various EU countries. They explore the disparities and commonalities of youth, shedding light on the various outcomes resulting from current migrant integration policies. These visualizations serve as a resource: for students wishing to better understand the situation but can also cater to researchers, humanitarian organizations, policymakers, and socially conscious individuals, fostering informed decision-making and engagement. Students are central to social movments within EU countries. The future of Europe is important to them. They care about social justice and human rights but often don't have access to meaningful visualisations. That is why this kind of platform matters to them. Understanding these patterns is pivotal in addressing the educational and employment challenges faced by migrant youth, allowing for people to demand the development of more effective support systems and inclusive policies. Currently, users can navigate the visualizations by selecting specific years, with flags representing different EU countries and their placement indicating key education and employment statistics. Additionally, the size of each flag corresponds to the migrant youth population, providing a visual representation of the differences between migrants and their experiences in each of the EU countries.
